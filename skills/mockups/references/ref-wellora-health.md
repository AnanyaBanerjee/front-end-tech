# Reference: Wellora (Health/Wellness App)

Source: App Store screenshot set, shared 2026-04-23

## What this set does well

- **Soft sage green throughout**: `#8DBF6E` / `#A8D48A` — warm, calming, health-appropriate
- **Multiple overlapping phones**: Slides 1, 4, 5 — 2–3 phones of different sizes overlap at slight angles
- **Serif/editorial typography**: Uses an italic serif or editorial-style font for feature headlines — premium feel
- **Press logos**: "GQ · Forbes · VOGUE · COSMOPOLITAN" — extremely effective credibility slide
- **Organic shape cutouts**: Green blob/leaf shapes behind phones and overlapping them
- **Testimonial + stars**: Two stacked quotes with different authors

## Color palette
- Primary: `#8DBF6E` (sage green)
- Background: `#f0f8eb` (very light green tint, near white) or solid sage green
- Text on light: `#1a1a1a` (near-black)
- Text on green: `#1a1a1a` or `#FFFFFF`
- Stars: `#F5A623` (warm orange/gold)

## Typography
- Mix of serif italic for features ("Live with energy", "Eat smart daily") and bold sans for numbers
- NOT all caps — editorial, wellness brand voice
- Headline: ~90px at 1320px canvas

## Layout patterns
- Slide 1: Light green bg + 2 overlapping phones (dark themed) + organic shape + "Health made simple"
- Slide 2: Green bg + organic shape + "Discover our 10 million community" + phone
- Slide 3: Light bg + press logos centered (GQ Forbes Vogue Cosmopolitan) + stars + 2 testimonials
- Slide 4: Green bg + multiple phones overlapping + "Live with energy"
- Slide 5: Green bg + phone stack + "Eat smart daily"

## Key takeaways
- Press logos slide (no phone, no feature) is one of the strongest credibility signals available
- Sage green / muted natural tones communicate wellness/health better than bright neons
- Organic/blob shapes (CSS clip-path or SVG) behind phones add visual interest cheaply
- Stacking 2+ phones at different angles/sizes shows app depth in one slide
- Gold/amber star ratings on green bg = warm, trusted, editorial feel
