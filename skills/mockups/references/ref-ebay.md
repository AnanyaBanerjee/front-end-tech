# Reference: eBay

Source: App Store screenshot set, shared 2026-04-23

## What this set does well

- **Real photography as hero**: Slide 1 = man with curly hair (lifestyle shot), no phone frame visible — just person + headline
- **Alternating light/dark per slide**: White → dark → white → dark (clean rhythm)
- **Very short copy**: 3–4 word headlines — "Shop new and pre-loved.", "Never miss a beat.", "Clear out and cash in."
- **Full-bleed person photo (no phone)**: Slide 2 is just a woman with headphones + tagline — no device at all
- **Notification card overlay**: Slide 2 has a floating promo card (`Give big with 15% off`) overlaid on photo
- **Dark mode slide**: Slide 5 shows phone in dark mode with "You don't run with the crowd" caption

## Color palette
- Light slides: `#ffffff` or `#f5f5f5`
- Dark slides: `#1a1a1a` or `#111`
- Accent: `#86357A` (deep purple, eBay promo card)
- Text: `#000000` on light, `#FFFFFF` on dark

## Typography
- Font: Heavy grotesque, title case
- Headline: ~110px at 1320px canvas, tight leading
- Copy: Factual and direct ("Real-time notifications for drops, offers, and more")

## Layout patterns
- Slide 1: White bg + person photo (partial bleed) + phone in lower half + headline top
- Slide 2: Dark bg + full-bleed person photo + floating offer card overlay
- Slide 3: White bg + phone centered + feature headline + bulleted steps (3 items)
- Slide 4: White bg + phone + person lifestyle photo inset in phone screen
- Slide 5: Dark bg + phone in dark mode + caption

## Key takeaways
- Real photography (not 3D renders) communicates trust for e-commerce/marketplace apps
- Floating notification/offer card overlay on lifestyle photo = compelling slide type
- Alternating white/dark slides is the cleanest rhythm for a 5-slide set
- Short factual copy outperforms clever copy at thumbnail scale
