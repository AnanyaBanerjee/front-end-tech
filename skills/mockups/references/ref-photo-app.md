# Reference: Photo/Image Discovery App (Unsplash-style)

Source: App Store screenshot set, shared 2026-04-23

## What this set does well

- **Full bleed photography as ALL backgrounds**: Every slide uses a high-quality photo as background — no solid color fills
- **Minimal text overlay**: Just headline + sometimes a subline — very clean
- **Tilted phone**: Slide 3 — phone shown at a ~15° tilt with perspective
- **Dark text on light photos, white on dark photos**: Typography adapts to photo content
- **No decorative elements**: Zero icons, badges, or overlays — pure confidence in the photos

## Color palette
- Variable (depends on photo)
- Text: `#FFFFFF` on dark photos, `#000000` or `#1a1a1a` on light photos
- Headline fills: Pure white or near-black

## Typography
- Headline: ALL CAPS, ~150px, heavy weight
- "FIND IMAGES QUICK", "BEAUTIFUL FREE PHOTOS", "GET THE BEST IMAGES"
- No subtitles on some slides — headline does all the work

## Layout patterns
- Slide 1: Woman on blue bg photo + "FIND IMAGES QUICK" + subtitle bottom
- Slide 2: Skateboarder mid-air photo — NO phone, full bleed, just headline text
- Slide 3: Dark phone bg photo + tilted phone (perspective) + app name
- Slide 4: White bg with phone centered + search UI visible
- Slide 5: Letterforms floating pattern + phone + "EXPLORE THE COLLECTION"

## Key takeaways
- For photo/visual apps, the screenshots ARE the product — let them fill the entire slide
- Tilted phone (perspective transform) on a photo background = most dynamic layout
- ALL CAPS single-word or 3-word headline is enough — no need for subtitles
- Pure photo slides (no phone) communicate what the product does immediately
