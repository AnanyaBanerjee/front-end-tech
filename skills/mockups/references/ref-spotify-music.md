# Reference: Spotify-style Music App

Source: App Store screenshot set, shared 2026-04-23

## What this set does well

- **Artist photography backgrounds**: Slides 3 and 5 use full bleed artist/lifestyle photos as bg — phone floats in front
- **Brand green on black**: Green `#1DB954` on black — Spotify's iconic combo
- **Phone on green bg**: Slides 1 and 4 — phone centered on solid green background, very clean
- **Large circle avatar**: Circular avatar/photo element behind phone on slide 1 (artist face peeking around phone edge)
- **Dot grid pattern**: Subtle dotted grid texture on some slides

## Color palette
- Brand: `#1DB954` (Spotify green)
- Background (slides): `#000000` or solid `#1DB954`
- Text on green: `#FFFFFF`
- Text on black: `#FFFFFF`

## Typography
- Headline: ~120px at 1320px canvas
- Style: Bold, title case — "Music, Podcasts & AudioBooks in One Place", "Find Artists You Love"
- Subtext: Smaller, ~48px

## Layout patterns
- Slide 1: Green bg + large phone centered + circular artist photo peeking behind
- Slide 2: Black bg + phone + artist photo in background (blurred)
- Slide 3: Full-bleed concert/artist photo + phone overlaid center + "Connect with Friends through your Playlist"
- Slide 4: Green bg + phone + "Search for Podcast" headline
- Slide 5: Dark/black bg + person photo + "Find Music for Your Soul"

## Key takeaways
- Circular artist/person photo peeking from behind the phone = clever layering technique
- Same brand green as both bg AND accent creates strongest brand recall
- Artist photography signals emotional product (music, entertainment) better than abstract design
- "X in One Place" is a strong benefit headline for multi-feature apps
