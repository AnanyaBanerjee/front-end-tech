# Reference: Fitness/Route Tracking App (Orange Theme)

Source: App Store screenshot set, shared 2026-04-23

## What this set does well

- **Slide 1**: Dark bg + 3D athlete figure (render/photo) + "Your Fitness Your Data" bold + award badges ("App of the Day", "10M+ Users")
- **Orange accent background**: `#E85200` fill on some slides — high energy, sports-appropriate
- **Award badges top of hero**: "App of the Day" wreath icon + "10M+ Users" count — credibility signals
- **Three-dot nav indicator**: Subtle white dots at bottom of slides (like carousel indicator) — professional touch
- **Challenges/groups feature highlight**: One slide shows the community/challenges feature — good for social proof

## Color palette
- Dark bg: `#0d0d0d`
- Orange: `#E85200` / `#FF5700`
- Text: `#FFFFFF`
- Orange bg text: `#FFFFFF`

## Typography
- Headline: Heavy, multi-line, dramatic
- "Your Fitness\nYour Data" — short declarative ownership statement
- "Track Runs Rides And Walks!" — actionable
- "Every Track Tells a Story" — emotional

## Layout patterns
- Slide 1: Dark bg + 3D sprinter figure filling left 60% + headline right + award badges
- Slide 2: Dark bg + phone centered + "Track Runs Rides And Walks!" + orange CTA pill
- Slide 3: Dark bg + phone showing routes map + "Smarter Every Workout"
- Slide 4: Orange bg + phone showing groups + "Every Track Tells a Story"
- Slide 5: Dark bg + 2 phones side by side

## Key takeaways
- 3D render of the product's target user (athlete) as hero visual = aspirational positioning
- "App of the Day" + "10M+ Users" in slide 1 = instant credibility before showing any feature
- Orange is underused in fitness — stands out against the sea of blue/green apps
- CTA pill button within the slide ("See Real Results") adds interactivity cue
